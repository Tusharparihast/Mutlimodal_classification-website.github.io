Ancient and Medieval Periods

Maithili Music: Originating from the Mithila region, which spans parts of Nepal and India, Maithili music is among the oldest in South Asia. Its exact inception is unclear, but its ancient roots suggest it influenced other musical traditions in the region.

Newar Music: The Newar community of the Kathmandu Valley developed a complex musical culture over two millennia, blending Hindu and Buddhist musical traditions. Instruments primarily include percussion and wind types.

Lichchhavi and Malla Periods

Dapha Music: Emerging in the late Lichchhavi period and flourishing during the Malla era, Dapha is a form of classical Newar devotional music based on classical ragas. It's performed by groups known as 'Dapha Khalah' and is integral to various rituals and festivals.

Shah and Rana Periods

Classical Music Patronage: During these times, classical music saw significant growth. Rulers like King Mahendra and King Birendra promoted classical music through palace concerts and radio broadcasts, embedding it deeply into Nepali culture.

Modern Era

Radio Nepal: Established in 1952, Radio Nepal became a pivotal platform for Nepali music, introducing and popularizing various genres across the country.

Contemporary Music: The late 20th century witnessed the rise of Nepali pop, especially among urban youth between 1985 and 2000. This era marked a blend of traditional sounds with modern musical styles.

Asare

Explore the rich heritage of Asare, a traditional Nepalese music style associated with the planting season.

Astamatrika

Astamatrika, representing eight mother goddesses, embodies the spiritual depth of Nepalese classical dance and music.

Basuri

The melodious tones of Basuri (flute) are a key component of Nepalese folk music, evoking the charm of rural life.

Bhairab Dance

Bhairab Dance is a dynamic performance symbolizing divine power and protection in traditional Nepalese festivals.

Bhote Lhomi

Bhote Lhomi is a traditional form of music from the ethnic Bhote community, known for its deep, expressive tunes and connection to the Himalayas.

Bhumi Nach

Bhumi Nach is a dance and music form that symbolizes the connection between humans and the earth, often performed during agricultural festivals.

Chaliya

Chaliya is a fast-paced dance and music form popular in the Terai region, typically performed during festivals and joyous occasions.

Charya

Charya is a ritualistic dance form of the Newar community, characterized by graceful movements and devotional music for religious ceremonies.

Chyabrung

Chyabrung is a traditional folk music from the Limbu ethnic group, featuring vibrant rhythms and energetic dance movements.

Damaha

Damaha is a traditional drum-based music form, often played during festivals and celebrations to create an energetic atmosphere.

Deuda

Deuda is a traditional form of folk music from the western regions of Nepal, performed during harvest festivals and cultural gatherings.

Dhami

Dhami is a traditional folk music genre of the Tamang community, performed to invoke blessings from deities during ceremonies.

Dhime

Dhime is a rhythmic drumbeat and musical style typically used in traditional rituals and performances to signify spiritual power.

Ghatu

Ghatu is a folk music style performed by women in the western hills of Nepal, often accompanied by a traditional dance.

Gunla

Gunla is a sacred music genre performed during the Gunla festival, dedicated to the worship of Lord Buddha.

Jhapre

Jhapre is a popular folk music genre in the eastern part of Nepal, known for its storytelling lyrics and communal singing.

Jhijhiya

Jhijhiya is a folk dance and music form from the Terai region, traditionally performed during the marriage season.

Jhora

Jhora is a lively music and dance form from the eastern hills, performed during festivals and celebrations.

Kaura

Kaura is a type of devotional music associated with the worship of gods and deities, often performed with traditional instruments in rituals.

Kartik

Kartik is a devotional music style sung during the Kartik festival, dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Khukuri

Khukuri is a traditional dance and music form performed by the Gurung and Magar communities. The music features vigorous beats on the dhol and other percussion instruments, often accompanying fast-paced dances.

Kumari

Kumari music is associated with the worship of the living goddess in Nepal. It includes chants, hymns, and rituals performed during ceremonies that honor the goddess. The music is deeply spiritual and ceremonial.

Lahare

Lahare is a type of traditional dance and music performed in the hills of Nepal. The music is marked by rhythmic drumming and accompanied by fast and lively footwork during community celebrations and religious rituals.

Lakhe

Lakhe is a masked dance drama performed during the festivals of the Newar community in Kathmandu Valley. The music includes chanting and drumming, creating a rhythmic and intense atmosphere during the dance.

Lathi

Lathi is a martial art and traditional dance performed by the people of the eastern Terai. The music typically involves fast-paced rhythms played on the madal and dholak, accompanying the movements of the dancers wielding sticks.

Mahakali

Mahakali music is part of the rituals dedicated to the goddess Mahakali in Hindu worship. It features traditional instruments like the damphu and dhime, creating a dramatic and intense atmosphere during the religious ceremonies.

Maruni

Maruni is a traditional folk music performed by women in the eastern Terai of Nepal. The music is lively and energetic, accompanied by dancing and singing, and is often performed during festivals and weddings.

Mayur

Mayur is a traditional form of folk dance and music performed in the eastern hills of Nepal. The music is characterized by rhythmic drumming and melodies, often symbolizing the peacock's movements in the dance.

Murchunga

Murchunga is a traditional Nepali folk music, known for its soulful melodies and unique instrument sounds.

Monkey Dance

Monkey Dance is a traditional dance form performed by the Newar community of the Kathmandu Valley. The dance mimics the movements of monkeys, with music featuring a lively rhythm played on drums and cymbals.

Palam

Palam is a traditional folk song performed during agricultural harvest seasons, celebrating the abundance of the land.

Panchebaja

Panchebaja is a traditional Nepali music ensemble consisting of five different instruments: the trumpet (tungna), the drum (dhol), the cymbals (manjeera), the flute (bansuri), and the harmonium. It is a key part of cultural rituals and festivals in the hilly regions of Nepal.

Rajbanshi

Rajbanshi music is an important part of the culture of the Rajbanshi people in the eastern Terai region of Nepal. It is characterized by rhythmic folk songs and dances, accompanied by traditional instruments like the dholak and madal.

Sakela

Sakela is a popular cultural dance and music form of the Kirat Rai community, widely performed during the Sakela festival. It involves dynamic movements and rhythmic beats played on traditional instruments like the madal, dhol, and flute.

Sarangi

Sarangi is a traditional Nepali string instrument known for its soulful and melancholic sound. It is widely used in folk music and storytelling, and often accompanies vocal performances, particularly in the mountain regions of Nepal.

Saraya

Saraya is a popular traditional dance music performed by the indigenous communities of the hills. It features fast-paced rhythms played on instruments such as the madal and dhol, and is often performed during festivals and social gatherings.

Sherpa

Sherpa music reflects the culture of the Sherpa people of Nepal, particularly in the Everest region. Their music features a mix of chanting and instrumental sounds, often accompanied by the sounds of the long horn and other traditional instruments.

Singaru

Singaru is a traditional folk song sung by the people of the Terai region in Nepal. The songs are often accompanied by the rhythms of the madal and dholak, expressing themes of love, nature, and daily life.

Sorathi

Sorathi is a type of Nepali folk music popular in the western parts of Nepal. It often involves lively rhythms and is performed with traditional instruments such as the madal and flute. It is known for its celebratory and danceable tunes.

Tharu

Tharu music is an important part of the cultural identity of the Tharu people, who primarily live in the Terai region of Nepal. The music features rhythmic beats on the dhol and madal, and is often accompanied by traditional dance during festivals and community events. Tharu songs celebrate their rural life and connection with nature.
